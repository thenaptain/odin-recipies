Nigerian Red Stew

Home

Description

The basic of this stew is fresh tomatoes, fresh bell peppers, and onions. These fresh vegetables compliment each other to give this stew a distinctive taste. You can serve Nigerian red tomato stew with white rice, beans, or even add a dollop to Nigerian moi-moi.

Ingredients

  • 1.5kg Mixed Goat Meat (available from good butchers)
  • 2 Red Bell Peppers Finely Chopped
  • 2/3 Tube of Tomato Paste
  • 2 Tins of Chopped Tomatoes
  • Onions
    • 2 Big Onions Chopped small
    • 1 Big Onion Cut into Quarters
  • 1 Large Bulb of Garlic Finely Chopped
  • 2-4 Scotch Bonnet Peppers chopped very fine* (Depending on your desired heat levels)
  • 5 tbsp West African Red Palm Oil
  • Glug of Vegetable oil
  • 2tsp Lion Brand Nigerian Curry Powder (or more if required)
  • 2tsp Dried Thyme (or more is required)
  • 8-10 Nigerian Knorr stock cubes

*Using Scissors to chop with fork if needed.

Method

  1. In a big pot, boil the meat in hot water with the onion cut into quarters, salt and about 3 or 4 maggi cubes. Let the meat boil for about 20 minutes.
  2. After boiling, strain the liquid but keep the liquid and then put the meat under the grill to crisp up a bit.
  3. While the meat is boiling, put the veg oil and palm oil together in another pot and fry it up for 5ish minutes - it will smoky as the palm oil evaporates - let this happen.
  4. Then put in chopped onions and garlic and scotch bonnet. Let that fry for a few minutes - lower the temperature so the onions and garlic fry gently until soft.
    • Add in tomatoes and bell peppers to the onion mixture and fry for about 15 minutes.
  5. Then pour some of the liquid from the boiled meat into the pot along with the curry powder & thyme & other maggi cubes and let cook for while - 20 minutes.
  6. Then add in the grilled meat and continue cooking for a while on a very low heat, while you are chilling with your beer (that last part is Bob's addition!!)
