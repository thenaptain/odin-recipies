Thai Fried Rice

Home

Description

Fried rice is a very typical Thai street food, available at every restaurant that serves stir fried dishes throughout the Thailand.

Ingredients

  • 400g Meat e.g. Boneless Chicken Thighs or King Prawns or soy chunks (No meat also works)
  • Eggs
    • 5 Eggs for rice
    • 1 Egg per person for frying
  • 2cups (When raw) of pre-cooked Golden Sella Basmati Rice
  • 2 Carrots finely diced
  • 6 Garlic Cloves and 6 Thai Chillies smashed
  • Kale
  • Desert spoon of sugar (light brown Muscavado if available)
  • Oyster Sauce
  • Fish Sauce
  • Thai 'thin" Soy Sauce
  • Oil

To Serve

  • Half a lime per person
  • Fresh Coriander
  • Chopped Thai chillies in a little pot of fish sauce

Method

Prepare ingredients first - chopping meat and veg, washing kale, smashing garlic & ginger, beating eggs for fried rice. It's quick cooking so it's good to have them ready. Best to use a non-stick wok for this.

  1. Bit of oil in wok - high heat
  2. Throw in chicken (if using) - chopped quite small. Cook fairly well, not just browning outside.
  3. Throw in carrots, garlic & chilli and give a good blitz - don't burn garlic (throw in green beans too if using)
  4. Throw in some of the rice - stir around and get it all coated by the chicken fat & oil - maybe half a wok full.
  5. Put all contents of wok in a big bowl and set aside.
  6. Make sure wok is free of rice - don't wash the wok though. Put in another tiny drop of oil, heat briefly and then throw in the beaten eggs.
  7. Stir around so kind of like scrambled egg (but not as scrambled) - don't overcook but as getting there, start to add back in the previous contents of the wok that had been set aside in a bowl. Give a good stir (play around with temperatures but it might be a good idea to lower slightly at this point).
  8. Add in a few glugs of soy sauce, fish sauce & oyster sauce - play around with how much. You don't want it to go brown with too much sauce added but equally you don't want it to be tasteless.
  9. Add in spoonful sugar
  10. Keep adding in more rice as needed and mixing together.
  11. Add more soy sauce, fish sauce & oyster sauce as necessary
  12. Start adding in kale bit by bit, stirring it into the rice mixture as it wilts and cooks.
  13. Set aside at a low temperature and now put some oil (generous amount) in a frying pan on a very very high heat.
  14. When oil is really at a high temperature, start frying your eggs for those eating (one per person) - you want to get a lovely crispy white and yet still with a fairly runny egg.
  15. Assemble nice big portion on a plate with fried egg on top, throw over some coriander and a lime wedge on the side. 16. Enjoy eating. Add in thai chillies or fish sauce from the little pot as you wish.
